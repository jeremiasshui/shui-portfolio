## I. Industry Context and Challenges

### 1.1 The Stark Reality of Global Food Loss

United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 12.3 explicitly mandates halving global food loss and waste by 2030. However, according to FAO statistics, one-third of all food produced worldwide continues to be lost within supply chains.

#### Comparative Food Loss Rates by Global Region

## II. Cold Chain Logistics: Core Concepts

### 2.1 Definition and Scope

**Cold Chain Logistics** refers to an integrated supply chain management system that maintains temperature-sensitive products within specified low-temperature environments throughout production, processing, storage, transportation, and distribution.

### 2.2 Temperature Classification Standards

Different products require distinct temperature parameters; precise temperature control determines cold chain success or failure.

### 2.3 The "Golden Rules" of Cold Chain Management

Industry-standard cold chain management principles can be distilled into the "3T Framework":

| Principle | Term | Description |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| **Time** | Time | Minimize product exposure to non-optimal temperature environments |
| **Temperature** | Temperature | Maintain specified temperature ranges throughout the entire process |
| **Tolerance** | Tolerance | Understand each product's threshold for temperature fluctuation |

> "The essence of cold chain lies not in 'cold,' but in 'continuity.' Temperature failure at any single point can render the entire chain's value null."

## V. Complete Cold Chain Operational Process

Cold chain logistics encompasses five major phases, each requiring strict temperature control to ensure chain integrity.

### 5.1 Process Overview

#### Cold Chain Logistics: Five-Phase Operational Standards

| Phase | Operational Content | Temperature Control Requirements | Key Performance Indicators |
|-------|---------------------|--------------------------------|---------------------------|

| **â‘¢ Cold Storage** | Warehouse storage, inventory management | Set per product specifications | Storage temperature fluctuation â‰¤ Â±1Â°C |
| **â‘£ Cold Chain Transport** | Loading, transit, unloading | Continuous temperature monitoring | Chain break incidents = 0 |
| **â‘¤ Last-Mile Delivery** | Final delivery to retail/consumer | Maintain unbroken cold chain | Delivery timeliness achievement rate â‰¥ 98% |

### 5.2 Phase-Specific Operational Guidelines

#### Phase One: Field Pre-Cooling

- Complete pre-cooling within **4 hours** of harvest
- Select pre-cooling method based on product characteristics:
  - Leafy vegetables â†’ Vacuum cooling (highest efficiency)
  - Fruits â†’ Forced-air cooling
  - Seafood â†’ Ice slurry or ice water cooling

#### Phase Two: Low-Temperature Processing and Packaging

- Maintain processing facility temperature at **15Â°C (59Â°F) or below**
- Employ Modified Atmosphere Packaging (MAP) to extend shelf life
- Select packaging materials considering both cold retention and environmental sustainability

#### Phase Three: Cold Storage

- Adhere to **First-In, First-Out (FIFO)** principles
- Maintain airflow channels between stacked goods
- Record temperature readings **minimum twice daily**

#### Phase Four: Cold Chain Transport

- Limit loading/unloading duration to **30 minutes or less**
- Pre-cool vehicle compartments to target temperature before loading
- Implement GPS positioning integrated with real-time temperature sensor feedback

#### Phase Five: Last-Mile Delivery

- Equip delivery containers with cold packs for temperature maintenance
- Verify product condition prior to consumer handoff
- Provide temperature history QR codes for customer verification

## IX. Conclusion

Cold chain logistics represents critical infrastructure for achieving United Nations Sustainable Development Goals and ensuring global food security. The core findings of this white paper may be summarized as follows:

### Key Insights

1. **Loss Problems Are Severe Yet Solvable**: Taiwan's 30% agricultural distribution loss rate can be reduced to below 15% through comprehensive cold chain implementation

2. **Policy Dividends Are Timely**: NT$12.6 billion in government investment creates opportunities for enterprises to leverage subsidies and reduce implementation barriers

3. **ROI Is Quantifiable**: Case studies demonstrate implementation benefits reaching 2-4x investment amounts

4. **Technology Barriers Are Declining**: Mature AIoT solutions make cold chain accessible even for small and medium enterprises

5. **Market Demand Is Clear**: Rising consumer expectations for food safety position cold chain traceability as a competitive advantage
